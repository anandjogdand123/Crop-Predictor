Nitrogen in Soil

Nitrogen is a vital nutrient for plant growth and is often deficient in soil. It is essential for the formation of amino acids, which are the building blocks of proteins.

Phosphorus in Soil

Phosphorus is also a crucial nutrient for plant growth, and is necessary for cell division and energy transfer. It is often found in soil in an insoluble form, which makes it difficult for plants to access.

Potassium in Soil

Potassium is essential for plant growth and is necessary for the proper functioning of many plant processes, including photosynthesis and water regulation. It is often deficient in soil and is typically added through fertilizers.
